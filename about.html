About Me

My journey into technology began in mechanical engineering, where I learned how machines move and interact with the physical world. Curious about how they can learn and adapt, I pursued a Master’s degree in computational engineering at Friedrich‑Alexander‑Universität Erlangen‑Nürnberg. Along the way I discovered my passion for artificial intelligence, robotics and generative models. I’ve worked on projects ranging from teleoperating robots and anonymising self‑driving data sets to extracting knowledge from scanned documents with large language models. Whether building a deep‑learning pipeline or designing an ETL workflow, my goal is always the same: turn raw data into reliable and ethical AI solutions.

When I’m not coding you’ll find me exploring reinforcement learning papers, experimenting with new frameworks or teaching friends about the latest advances in generative AI. I’m always looking for opportunities to collaborate on challenging projects that bridge the gap between hardware and intelligence.
